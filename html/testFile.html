CONs

When exhibiting at trade fairs, there are two important factors to consider in relation to your IP. Can you guess what they are?

    IP rights are territorial. Having a patent, a design, or a trade mark registered in the EU does not mean that you are protected worldwide. Registration is needed in each and every country, especially in South-East Asia where IP infringement is still a major concern.

    Trade fairs are made up of local and foreign exhibitors and visitors. Exhibiting IP owners are disclosing their IP to a much wider audience. For example, if the trade fair is in Singapore but the majority of the visitors are from China, it will be important to explore IP protection in China as well as in Singapore.

GEOGRAPHICAL ISSUES

        Budget will, of course, be a consideration when SMEs decide where to file for IP protection, but it is strongly advised that, if you are unsure, spending a few hundred euros to file a trade mark or a design application (a little more for a patent) is certainly worthwhile.
